Our Story

Hariyali Social Empowerment Foundation is a continuation of Hariyali Samsarika Samiti, which has been actively contributing to social welfare since 2011.

Our foundation is dedicated to fostering educational and economic empowerment in communities, particularly focusing on supporting underprivileged segments of society.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower individuals through education, skill development, and economic support, fostering equality and sustainability for all to reach their potential.

Our Vision

Our vision at Hariyali Foundation is to build a society where everyone can thrive through education and economic independence, creating better communities.

Our Projects

Education Promotion

As part of our commitment to education, we have launched a school kit distribution project in collaboration with the National NGO Confederation, Social Bee Ventures, and Sixteen of Maranchery. This initiative provides essential study materials to one thousand students with a 50% subsidy, ensuring that children from both Above Poverty Line (APL) and Below Poverty Line (BPL) families have the resources they need to succeed in their studies.

A Drop of Hope

Thrissur Police Academy Inspector TK Muhammad Basheer inaugurated a blood donation camp organized by Sixteen of Marancherry and Purung Medical Center in collaboration with Hariyali Sanskarika Samiti and Perinthalmanna Government Blood Bank. Marancherry Gram Panchayat Member Adv. KA Bakar presided over the event.

Women on Wheels

To promote women‚Äôs empowerment, we have initiated the ‚ÄúWomen on Wheels‚Äù project. This program, in partnership with the National NGO Confederation, Sixteen of Marancherry, and the Hariyali Cultural Committee, provides two-wheelers to 111 women working in various fields with 50% financial assistance. This initiative aims to enhance women‚Äôs mobility and independence, enabling them to pursue their careers and personal goals more effectively.

Machines of Change

Under the Social Entrepreneurship Development Project led by the National NGO Confederation, Hariyali Sanskari Samiti and Sixteen of Marancheri, a total of 568 Usha sewing machines were distributed to families at a 50% subsidy. In the third phase, 478 families received sewing machines, while 90 families were provided with sewing motors. With more than 300 families already benefiting in previous phases, this initiative continues to promote self-reliance and economic empowerment across the community.

Unlocking Potential

Our Skill Development Workshops, in partnership with industry leaders, training institutions, and career coaches, provide practical training in key areas like digital literacy, entrepreneurship, and vocational skills. Designed to enhance employability, these workshops equip individuals with the knowledge and hands-on experience needed to succeed in the workforce and pursue meaningful careers.
